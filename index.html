About

My Journey

It all started when I moved into my new house. I needed a desk for my computer and wanted something made of solid wood. Like any good homeowner, I turned towards Ikea for ideas.

The internet suggest that the Ikea Gerton would be a good solid top and so I went forward with it but found out that the wood was unfinished. I began looking up ways to finish the top and came across lots of tutorials and settled with a polyurethan finish. I had to get working on the top, sanding it down then applying multiple layers. After doing so, I had a good sense of accomplishment and in order to keep this going I decided that I would build as much furniture as I could.

Down the tool buying rabbit hole I entered and slowly I had a few projects to be proud of. After of course investing way more than necessary, I had to keep on building to make all the purchases worth it. Sorry wallet.

Below are some of the projects that I've completed. Not every build is very well documented as I only started taking more pictures of the building process when I decided to make this website.

Hopefully there will be more to come because... why buy something premade when I can spend double the price and triple the amount of time to build it myself!

Coffee Table

My couch badly needed a coffee table so I decided to make one from scratch. I wanted to cut some cost so I made the legs out of pine which I was able to find laying around my neighbourhood dumpster as the area is still a construction zone. The walnut top is not cheap! but came out beautifully once I applied the oil finish on it.

Click buttons below to see some assembly pictures of the coffee table

Wood: Walnut (top) pine (legs)

Finish: Osmos 1101 and Osmos 3034 (top) Spray paint and polyurethane (legs)
